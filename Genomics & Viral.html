Genomics & Viral Pathogens in Edible Cricket Farming

Exploring mitochondrial genome assembly, phylogeny, and viral threats to Acheta domesticus for sustainable cricket production.

Start Learning

Mitochondrial Genome Assembly & Phylogeny of Acheta domesticus

The mitochondrial genome is a key genetic marker for species identification and evolutionary studies. It is maternally inherited and evolves faster than nuclear DNA.

This study sequenced and assembled the complete mitochondrial genome of Acheta domesticus, the house cricket widely farmed globally.

Phylogenetic analysis using Bayesian inference and Principal Component Analysis (PCA) confirmed its distinct evolutionary position.

Mitochondrial Genome Assembly Workflow

1. DNA Extraction and Quality Check
Extract genomic DNA from adult Acheta domesticus specimens. Check quality and quantity using gel electrophoresis and spectrophotometry.
2. Next-Generation Sequencing (NGS)
Perform high-throughput sequencing using Illumina or similar platforms to generate paired-end reads.
3. Sequence Quality Control
Trim adapters and low-quality reads using software like Trimmomatic or FastQC.
4. Mitochondrial Genome Assembly
Use de novo assemblers (e.g., NOVOPlasty, MitoZ) to assemble mitochondrial genome from quality-filtered reads.
5. Genome Annotation
Identify protein-coding genes, tRNAs, and rRNAs using MITOS or similar annotation tools.
6. Phylogenetic and PCA Analysis
Construct phylogenetic trees using Bayesian methods. Perform PCA on mitochondrial sequence variation for population differentiation.

Acheta domesticus Volvovirus (AdVVV): Viral Threat to Cricket Farming

Viral diseases cause major losses in cricket farms. The newly identified Acheta domesticus volvovirus (AdVVV) infects house crickets.

This study detected AdVVV in Thai farms. It assembled and analyzed the viral genome and studied genetic diversity among isolates.

PCR tests confirmed infection in breeding stocks, highlighting biosecurity risks.

Viral Genome Detection & Characterization Workflow

1. Sample Collection & DNA Extraction
Collect samples from farmed Acheta domesticus populations. Extract total DNA including viral DNA.
2. Virus Detection by BLAST
Search cricket genome assemblies using BLAST to identify viral sequences related to volvovirus.
3. PCR Validation
Design primers and perform PCR on DNA samples to confirm presence of viral DNA.
4. Overlapping Amplicon Sequencing
Amplify overlapping viral genome fragments by PCR and sequence them.
5. Genome Assembly and Annotation
Assemble the complete viral genome. Annotate open reading frames (ORFs) and identify gene functions.
6. Phylogenetic and Genetic Variability Analysis
Build phylogenetic trees of viral isolates and analyze genetic variability among ORFs, especially capsid protein genes.

Key Findings

  • AdVVV genome is 2,516 nucleotides long with four open reading frames.
  • Thai isolate closely related to Japanese, but distinct from American isolates.
  • ORF1, encoding capsid protein, showed high genetic variability.
  • Viral monitoring and control are vital for farm health and sustainability.

Summary & Conclusion

Mitochondrial genome analysis provides tools for correct species ID and breeding. Viral study reveals a new pathogen threatening cricket farms. Both improve knowledge for sustainable cricket farming.

Suggested Learning Activities

  • Assemble and annotate mitochondrial genomes with bioinformatics tools.
  • Build and analyze phylogenetic trees from genetic data.
  • Explore biosecurity and viral disease management in insect farming.
  • Study viral genetic diversity and its impact on pathogen evolution.
