1. Abstract

Tense Guidelines:

  • Past tense: For methods and results
  • Present tense: For conclusions and implications
‚úì Correct Example:
"We investigated the role of protein X in cell division. Cell cultures were treated with inhibitor Y for 24 hours. Results showed a 50% reduction in mitotic activity. These findings suggest that protein X plays a crucial role in cellular proliferation."

2. Introduction

Tense Guidelines:

  • Present tense: For established facts and current knowledge
  • Present perfect: For recent developments

‚Ä¢ "Recent studies have revealed new mechanisms..." (recent development)

  • Past tense: For all procedures performed

Tense Guidelines:

  • Present tense: For established knowledge

üé≠ Active vs. Passive Voice in Biology Writing

When to Use Passive Voice

Preferred in:
  • Methods sections
  • When the action is more important than the actor
  • When the actor is obvious or irrelevant
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "Specimens were preserved in ethanol."
‚Ä¢ "The hypothesis was tested using..."
‚Ä¢ "Results were analyzed using SPSS."

When to Use Active Voice

Preferred in:
  • When emphasizing the researcher's role
  • For clarity and directness
  • In discussion and conclusion sections
Examples:
‚Ä¢ "We propose a new mechanism for..."
‚Ä¢ "This study demonstrates that..."
‚Ä¢ "Our findings suggest that..."

Voice Transformation Examples

‚ùå Problematic

"The experiment was performed by us."
"Mistakes were made in the analysis."
"The conclusion was reached by the researchers."

‚úì Improved

"We made mistakes in the analysis."
"We concluded that..."

Oral Presentation Tense Guidelines

Presentation Section Recommended Tense Example

Exercise 1: Tense Identification

Instructions: Identify the correct tense for each sentence based on the manuscript section.

  1. Chlorophyll _____ (absorb/absorbed/absorbs) light energy. (Introduction)
  2. We _____ (analyze/analyzed/have analyzed) the data using statistical software. (Methods)
  3. Figure 3 _____ (show/showed/shows) the correlation between variables. (Results)
  4. Recent studies _____ (reveal/revealed/have revealed) new insights. (Introduction)
  5. Our findings _____ (suggest/suggested/will suggest) a new mechanism. (Discussion)

Exercise 2: Voice Conversion

Instructions: Convert these sentences to appropriate voice for scientific writing.

  1. "We performed PCR amplification." ‚Üí (Convert to passive for Methods section)
  2. "The samples were analyzed by the research team." ‚Üí (Convert to active for clarity)
  3. "Errors were made in the calculation." ‚Üí (Convert to active for accountability)

Exercise 3: Section-Specific Writing

Instructions: Write a sentence for each section using appropriate tense and voice.

  • Abstract: Describe a completed experiment and its implications
  • Methods: Describe a laboratory procedure
  • Results: Report a finding while referring to a figure
  • Discussion: Interpret your results in context of existing knowledge

üîë Answer Key

Exercise 1 Answers:

  1. absorbs (present - established fact)
  2. analyzed (past - completed method)
  3. shows (present - describing current figure)
  4. have revealed (present perfect - recent developments)
  5. suggest (present - current interpretation)

Exercise 2 Answers:

  1. "PCR amplification was performed."
  2. "The research team analyzed the samples." or "We analyzed the samples."
  3. "We made errors in the calculation."

üìö Key Takeaways for Teachers

  • Consistency is crucial: Maintain appropriate tenses within each section
  • Voice depends on purpose: Use passive for methods, active for interpretations
  • Present for facts: Established knowledge should be in present tense
  • Past for actions: Completed procedures and findings use past tense
  • Practice with real examples: Use published papers from your field for exercises
  • Presentations differ: More present tense acceptable in oral presentations
