Step 1: Cell Collection
Duration: ~5 minutes
Collect biological sample containing cells with DNA. Common sources include cheek swabs, blood samples, or plant tissue.
Step Details

Objective:

Obtain a biological sample containing cells with intact DNA.

Method:

Use a sterile swab to collect cells from the inside of the cheek or collect blood/tissue samples.

Key Points:

  • Ensure sample sterility
  • Collect sufficient cell material
  • Store at appropriate temperature
Scientific Principles
Cell Structure:
Eukaryotic cells contain DNA in the nucleus, protected by nuclear and cell membranes.
DNA Organization:
DNA is packaged with histone proteins into chromatin within the nucleus.

DNA Extraction Principles

Overview:

DNA extraction is a molecular biology technique used to isolate DNA from cells. The process involves breaking down cellular structures and separating DNA from other cellular components.

Key Steps:

  1. Cell Lysis: Breaking cell and nuclear membranes
  2. Protein Denaturation: Removing histone and other proteins
  3. DNA Precipitation: Using alcohol to precipitate DNA
  4. Purification: Washing and resuspending pure DNA

Applications:

  • Genetic testing and diagnostics
  • Forensic analysis
  • Research and biotechnology
  • Evolutionary studies
  • Medical research

Quality Factors:

  • Sample freshness and storage
  • Contamination prevention
  • Proper buffer pH and ionic strength
  • Temperature control
  • Complete protein removal
